It&#8217;s funny, I&#8217;ve always been interested in how to make a curry that comes out like they do in the Indian restaurants- smooth, rich and creamy. &nbsp;I tried so many&nbsp;and they all came out tasting powdery or watery, something was never quite right. &nbsp;However,&nbsp;the other day I had a craving for a curry and thought I&#8217;d try again. of course now my requirements are not only &#8220;smooth, rich and creamy&#8221; but also gluten free, paleo friendly, keto friendly and sugar free (believe it or not, some recipes call for sugar!).

With all of those so called &#8220;restrictions&#8221; and winging it a little bit as far as ingreidents go (although I did use [this recipe](http://www.preppypaleo.com/2012/09/paleo-chicken-korma.html) as a rough guideline), I ended up making one of the best curries I&#8217;ve ever tasted!&nbsp; To get a glimpse of how happy I am with this recipe, I ate the entire batch the first day. &nbsp;Then made it again the next day. &nbsp;Then again the day after that. &nbsp;I figured 4 days in a row would be a bit much so I left it there but believe me when I tell you this is one of those recipes that tastes so good it should be a guilty pleasure, and yet&nbsp;it slips right in to any healthy lifestyle choice ðŸ™‚

I&#8217;ll stop going on now and leave you to&nbsp;find out just how good this one is for yourself ðŸ˜€ &nbsp;Enjoy!
