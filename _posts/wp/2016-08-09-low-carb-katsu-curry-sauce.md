I&#8217;m a big fan Asian foods. &nbsp;Thai. &nbsp;Chinese. &nbsp;Japanese. &nbsp;They&#8217;re so flavourful & spicy and generally pretty straightforward to make. &nbsp;They also make for great meal prep ideas üôÇ &nbsp;However this particular recipe was a first for me, and there&#8217;s a story behind it..

I made a bet with a certain someone that should I lose the bet I would cook them a gluten free dinner. &nbsp;Of course I lost the bet üòè&nbsp; So there I am thinking this will be easy, I make gluten free dinners everyday, I have such a wide repertoire to choose from, I could do this in my sleep.. &nbsp;Then comes the request that what I should cook is Japanese food. &nbsp;And not just that but I&#8217;m not just cooking for one person, I&#8217;m&nbsp;cooking for the whole family üòê &nbsp;Not one to shy away from a&nbsp;challenge I had 3 days to create, experiment with and perfect a Japanese dish &#8211; and so I decided upon&nbsp;Katsu curry.

Now while the deal was a gluten free dinner, I was also going to be eating it so it also had to fall under my preference&nbsp;of&nbsp;low carb. &nbsp;Naturally I went online and searched&nbsp;numerous Katsu curry sauces, all of which either a &#8211; had too many ingredients or b &#8211; heavily relied on sugar. &nbsp;If you hadn&#8217;t realised by now my philosophy on cooking is if it takes too many steps or it has too many ingredients it&#8217;s not something I&#8217;m going to make. &nbsp;The trick to eating healthy consistently I&#8217;ve found is to either have food on hand, prepped and ready to eat or it&#8217;s to be able to whip something up quickly enough before that little nagging voice in your head starts telling you there&#8217;s much faster ways to get fed right now! &nbsp;Therefore too many ingredients or too many steps is too much for me üôÇ

So I did what I always do in this situation &#8211; find the top 3 or 4 recipes that look like they&#8217;d make a meal, pick the ingredients I like from them, and use that as a base to create my own recipe.. &nbsp;And that is what you are looking at right now üòÄ &nbsp;This is low carb, it has very few ingredients, very little effort is involved & it can be prepped ready to be poured onto grilled chicken or veg or courgetti or anything else you fancy.. Instant curry! &nbsp;So with this now being the longest post I&#8217;ve written so far.. &nbsp;Go forth & make Katsu!

(PS: &nbsp;The dinner came off without a hitch & everyone really enjoyed the sauce &#8211; even after I explained it was low carb üòÅ)
